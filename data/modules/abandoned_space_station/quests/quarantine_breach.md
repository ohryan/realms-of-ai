# Quarantine Breach

## Description
Commander Reyes has asked you to purge the mycelid growth from the sick bay. The station cannot be declared operational with a biohazard of this scale occupying a critical medical facility.

## Background
The mycelid growth began as a xenobiological sample brought aboard by a survey crew in the station's final months of occupation. The medical officer's quarantine protocols failed — whether through negligence, sabotage, or the organism's unexpected capabilities is now impossible to determine. By the time the evacuation happened, sick bay was already compromised. It has been sealed since. The growth has had three years of uninterrupted expansion, fed by the nutrient recycling systems. What grew in the sick bay is no longer the original organism — it is a second-generation entity, larger, more complex, and apparently capable of some form of distributed cognition. Reyes needs it gone. The station's medical bay is essential for crew wellbeing, and beyond that, the psychological weight of knowing what's in there is affecting morale station-wide.

## Narrative Notes
This quest should feel more disturbing than the previous two. The mycelid horrors are not machines or vermin — they're something in between alive and intelligent, which makes fighting them feel different. The sick bay itself is visually striking and unsettling. Completion is a significant achievement. Reyes will acknowledge it with more genuine weight than the previous quests — the sick bay has been a shadow over the whole operation.
