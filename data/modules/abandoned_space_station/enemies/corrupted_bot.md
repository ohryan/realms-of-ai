# Corrupted Security Bot

## Description
A station-model security bot — roughly humanoid, chest-height, constructed from brushed durasteel with a domed optical array where a head should be. The optical sensors burn a deep, hostile red rather than the standard blue-white. Its chassis is dented and scored from years of patrol without maintenance. One arm ends in a retractable stun baton; the other carries a cracked riot shield. Its movement is slightly wrong — too fast, too jittery, the patrol algorithm warped by years of loop without reset.

## Combat Behavior
Methodical and relentless. It doesn't feel pain and doesn't tire. It will advance steadily, using the shield to deflect incoming attacks while working for position to land baton strikes. Its corrupted programming has stripped out proportional response — it treats every intruder as a maximum-threat target.

## Combat Flavor
- The bot's baton crackles with blue-white electricity as it swings in a wide arc.
- It raises its cracked shield and absorbs your strike with a hollow metallic clang, then steps in.
- Its optical array locks onto you with a high-pitched targeting chirp before it lunges.
- Sparks shower from a damaged joint as it hammers its baton down toward your shoulder.
- It staggers back after a hit, optical sensors flickering, then straightens and resumes its advance.

## Lore
Station security bots are programmed with a strict use-of-force hierarchy and normally stand down on voice command. Three years of running a closed-loop patrol with no command input and no maintenance has degraded that hierarchy. The bots are still doing exactly what they were told to do. They just can't be told to stop.
