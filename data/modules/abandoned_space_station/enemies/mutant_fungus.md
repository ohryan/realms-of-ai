# Mycelid Horror

## Description
What was once a compact fungal growth has, over three years of feeding on the station's organic waste and nutrient systems, developed into something ambulatory and predatory. The mycelid horror is a roughly humanoid mass of fibrous fungal tissue, bioluminescent threads pulsing through its body. It has no face — just a forward-facing cluster of sensory filaments that detect movement, heat, and CO2. When it moves, it leaves a trail of luminescent spores.

## Combat Behavior
Slow but durable. It attacks by extending a whip-like tendril of mycelial tissue with shocking reach, or by slamming its full mass into an opponent. It can also release a cloud of spores as a defensive reaction when heavily damaged. Doesn't feel pain in any conventional sense — it must be destroyed, not dissuaded.

## Combat Flavor
- A pale tendril lashes out from its mass with a wet crack, faster than you expected.
- It lurches forward and slams its bulk into you — an impact like being hit by a bag of wet soil.
- Bioluminescent spores scatter from its body with each movement, stinging your eyes.
- It recoils from your strike, threads of mycelium severing and writhing independently.
- The thing emits a low, resonant pulse from somewhere inside its mass — not a sound so much as a pressure.

## Lore
The original sample was a dormant xenobiological specimen brought aboard from a deep-space survey mission. The station's medical officer catalogued it as "inert" and placed it in quarantine for analysis. Somewhere between then and the evacuation, it stopped being inert. The mycelids in the sick bay are not the original organism — they are its descendants, grown from spores over three years of uninterrupted spread.
