# Void Stalker

## Description
A void stalker is built for killing. Roughly two meters long with a low, quadrupedal stance, it's covered in a matte black carapace that absorbs light rather than reflecting it. Its head is elongated and eyeless — it navigates entirely by vibration and thermal sensing. Four limbs end in hooked claws designed for gripping metal surfaces; a fifth appendage, a bladed tail, trails behind it. It moves in complete silence until it strikes. The alpha variant is larger, battle-scarred, and considerably more dangerous.

## Combat Behavior
Calculated and patient. It prefers to circle, testing for weaknesses, before committing to an attack. When it strikes, it's explosive and committed — all-out until the threat is neutralized or it retreats to reassess. The alpha does not retreat. It will use its tail as a whip to create distance before closing again.

## Combat Flavor
- It erupts from shadow with a sound like tearing metal, claws raking for your face.
- The bladed tail sweeps low in a wide arc, forcing you to jump back or take it across the shins.
- Its carapace-head slams forward in a brutal headbutt that carries the weight of its whole body.
- It pivots faster than anything its size should move, hooking a claw through your guard.
- A low subsonic vibration emanates from its chest — a threat display, a half-second before it charges.

## Lore
Void stalkers are not on any known xenobiological registry. They did not come from a survey sample or a known alien world. The station's sensor logs show no indication of how they arrived, only that their thermal signatures first appeared in the lower decks approximately six months after the evacuation. Whatever brought them here did not come through the docking bay.
