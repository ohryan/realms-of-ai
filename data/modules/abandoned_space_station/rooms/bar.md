# The Void's Edge Bar

## Description
Against all odds, the bar is open. Nova has the counter wiped down, the amber lighting restored, and a row of synthetic spirits lined up behind the taps. A handful of off-duty crew and independent contractors occupy the booths and barstools. The place has a makeshift charm — mismatched chairs, salvaged decor, a hand-painted sign above the counter reading "THE VOID'S EDGE." Voss's trading crates are stacked in the far corner, half of them open for business.

## Atmosphere
The bar is the social nucleus of the re-inhabitation effort. It's the one place on the station that feels genuinely alive. People come here to forget that the lower decks are still sealed, that the sick bay is quarantined, that they're three weeks from the nearest inhabited system. The music is something low and synthetic, played from a battered speaker behind the counter.

## Notable Features
- A hand-painted "THE VOID'S EDGE" sign above the bar counter
- Rows of synthetic spirits and a working tap system
- Mismatched salvaged furniture — no two chairs the same
- Voss's open trading crates in the far corner
- A flickering holoscreen above the bar cycling station news and crew announcements
- A dartboard on one wall with "ENGINEERING BOTS" scrawled under the bullseye

## Secrets
Nova keeps a locked cabinet under the bar with pre-evacuation bottles — real grain alcohol, not synthesized. She doesn't advertise them. She knows more about what happened the first time this station was evacuated than she lets on.

## Sounds and Smells
Low synthetic music. The clink of glasses. Murmured conversation. The smell of industrial solvent cologne and something almost like real food cooking in the back.
