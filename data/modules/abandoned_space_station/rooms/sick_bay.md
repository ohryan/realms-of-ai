# Sick Bay

## Description
The sick bay is almost unrecognizable as a medical facility. Every surface is covered in a thick, pale mycelid growth — fibrous networks of fungal matter draped over examination tables, equipment cabinets, and the walls themselves. Bioluminescent spores drift lazily through the air. At the center of the room, two pulsing masses of fungal growth have developed grotesque pseudo-limbs. They are very much alive — and aware of your presence.

## Atmosphere
This room was once immaculate. Now it looks like the inside of a living thing. The equipment — surgical arms, diagnostic pods, a pharmaceutical dispensary — is buried under layers of organic matter. The growth has been here a long time, fed by the station's nutrient recyclers and undisturbed darkness. There is something almost beautiful about it, in the way a disaster can be beautiful.

## Notable Features
- Thick mycelid growth covering all surfaces
- Bioluminescent spores drifting slowly through the air
- Buried examination tables visible beneath the organic mass
- A pharmaceutical dispensary cabinet, mostly consumed by growth
- Emergency oxygen masks hanging on the far wall — still accessible

## Secrets
Beneath the largest mass of growth, a medical officer's personal log is entombed in amber-like fungal resin. It documents the outbreak spreading from a soil sample brought aboard from a deep-space mining survey. The last entry reads: "It's not just growing. It's thinking."

## Sounds and Smells
A wet, pulsing sound — like slow breathing. The creak of fungal tendrils shifting. The smell is overwhelmingly earthy and sweet, almost nauseating. Breathing here without a mask would not be advisable.
