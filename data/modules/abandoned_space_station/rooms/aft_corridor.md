# Aft Corridor

## Description
A utilitarian corridor running aft of the habitation ring. Exposed conduit runs along the ceiling. The plating underfoot is a heavy diamond-tread steel, scuffed and dented from years of heavy cargo movement. The main corridor is accessible via ladder above; the bar is below; and a sealed pressure door leads south toward the cargo bay.

## Atmosphere
Nobody lingers here. It's a through-space — functional and grim, designed for cargo handlers and maintenance crews, not comfort. The re-inhabitation team has marked the cargo bay door with a handwritten note: "VERMIN — Checked in. Not checked out." The door is still unlocked.

## Notable Features
- Exposed conduit and pipe runs along the ceiling
- Heavy diamond-tread deck plating
- A handwritten warning note taped to the cargo bay door: "VERMIN — Checked in. Not checked out."
- A manual pressure release wheel on the cargo bay door

## Secrets
The pressure door to the cargo bay seals from the outside. Whoever was in the cargo bay last didn't seal it behind them — which means they either left in a hurry or didn't leave at all.

## Sounds and Smells
The groan of the station's hull under micro-thermal stress. Faint scrabbling sounds from the direction of the cargo bay. The smell of machine oil and cold metal.
