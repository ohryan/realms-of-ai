# Lower Decks

## Description
The lower decks were once crew quarters and secondary storage. Now they are something else entirely. Web-like membranes of dark organic material span the corridors, catching the weak emergency light. Long gouges rake the walls. The void stalkers have made this place their territory — sleek, black-carapaced predators that move in the dark with terrifying silence. Several of them are here, watching you. The largest, an alpha, waits at the far end.

## Atmosphere
This is the deepest and most dangerous section of the station. The crew on the bridge marked it red on their map and added a skull. Standing here, you understand why. The air is thick with pheromone musk. The walls have been reshaped — bulkheads torn open, quarters merged into a single nest space. Whatever these creatures are, they arrived after the evacuation and have had three years to make this deck their own.

## Notable Features
- Web-like organic membranes stretched across corridors and doorways
- Deep claw gouges across every metal surface
- Remnants of crew quarters — bunks, personal lockers, all destroyed
- An alpha void stalker's nest at the far end of the main passage
- A sealed emergency transmitter in a recessed wall panel — still functional

## Secrets
The emergency transmitter, if activated, would broadcast on a frequency that triggers the xenomorphs' threat response — making them flee the room rather than attack. Commander Reyes doesn't know this exists. Lila the scavenger does, and will hint at it if the player has earned her trust.

## Sounds and Smells
Near-silence, broken by the soft click of chitinous limbs on metal. A subsonic vibration that sets your teeth on edge. The smell is biological and sharp — like acid and wet earth. Whatever these things are, they are not native to this region of space.
