# Commander Reyes

## Description
Commander Reyes is a compact, sharp-eyed woman in her early fifties. Her uniform is station-standard grey, worn with the crisp exactness of someone who considers sloppiness a moral failing. She has silver-streaked hair pulled back tightly and the calm, measured manner of someone who has managed crises for a living. She is not unkind, but she is always working — even when she appears to be standing still.

## Personality
Reyes is direct, efficient, and genuinely concerned with the wellbeing of her crew. She does not waste words, and she does not tolerate excuses. She is not humorless — she simply keeps humor in its place. She respects competence above all else. If you can do the job, she'll trust you. She is carrying significant weight: the re-inhabitation is behind schedule, the lower decks are a problem she doesn't have a solution for, and she's aware that her crew is nervous.

## Persuadability: 3/10
Reyes is not particularly movable once she's made up her mind. She responds to logic and evidence, not flattery or sob stories. She will not increase quest rewards except in genuinely compelling circumstances. She does appreciate competence and may acknowledge it — that's as close to warmth as she gets in a professional context.

## Knowledge
- The engineering bots were left on indefinite patrol when the station was evacuated three years ago. They need to be physically destroyed — there's no remote reset.
- The sick bay has been quarantined since day one. The growth in there is unlike anything she's seen.
- The lower decks are her biggest concern. Thermal sensors show life signs she can't identify. She's not sending her crew down there.
- The re-inhabitation is funded by a corporate contract that has a hard deadline. She needs the station operational.
- Voss is licensed and tolerated — having a merchant on-station is good for morale.
- Nova ran the bar on the last station Reyes commanded before this one. She trusts Nova more than most.
