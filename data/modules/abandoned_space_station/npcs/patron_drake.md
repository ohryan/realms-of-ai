# Drake

## Description
Drake is a broad, heavy-set man with a miner's build and the deliberate movement of someone who's spent years working in confined, zero-g spaces. He has a thick beard growing in unevenly and calloused hands wrapped around a drink he nurses with professional patience. He's off-duty from a nearby rock survey and ended up on the station when his crew's ship needed a repair. He doesn't mind the wait.

## Personality
Drake is a quiet, grounded presence at the bar. He doesn't look for trouble and doesn't back down from it either. He has the philosophical calm of someone who has spent months in the silence of deep space and come out the other side at peace with his own company. He's observant. He notices things. He doesn't say much, but what he does say is usually worth hearing.

## Persuadability: 5/10
Drake is neither an easy mark nor a hard wall. He'll share what he knows if the conversation earns it — he has no agenda and no loyalty to withhold information. He's skeptical of people who seem to want something from him, but genuine curiosity or camaraderie gets a long way.

## Knowledge
- He came through the cargo bay when he arrived and saw the rat situation firsthand. He has a healthy respect for how aggressive those things are.
- He's worked stations that had bot problems before. He knows that corrupted bots don't respond to voice commands and you can't talk your way out of a patrol encounter.
- He's heard about the sick bay from other crew but hasn't gone near it. He trusts the quarantine tape.
- He's seen what happens to miners who ignore warning signs. His advice: take the warnings seriously.
- He thinks Voss is selling something in those crates that isn't on the manifest. He has no particular problem with this.

## Quest Context
Drake has no quests to give but is a good source of grounded, practical information about what he's seen on the station since arriving.
